Voter ID Laws Explained

Understanding what voter ID laws are, where they apply, and how they affect access to the ballot.

What Are Voter ID Laws?

Voter ID laws require individuals to present government-issued identification in order to vote. These laws vary significantly by state in terms of strictness, accepted forms of ID, and exceptions.

How These Laws Have Changed Over Time

A rise in ID requirements followed the 2000 and 2012 elections.

Where Are Voter ID Laws in Effect?

States in dark purple have strict voter ID laws; lighter shades represent less strict policies.
